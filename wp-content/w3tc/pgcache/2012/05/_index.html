The i9 Party Continues

Chicago Battle @ Elbo room, The HuB w/ JWP, Caterwaulla CD release

 

It’s been a crazy past few weeks.

We got some more press.  Thanks to the Gazette and Hoopla!  Check it:
http://hooplanow.com/2012/05/10/item-9-and-the-mad-hatters-is-a-local-rock-band-worth-following/

On April 30th, a Monday night, item9 and the gang journeyed once again to the Elbo Room in Chicago for a Rock Show. This time the stakes were high, it was a battle. We got to Chi town, ditched our bags in the hotel, then headed downtown. The owner was pretty happy to see us as a few bands had already dropped out. We watched the first band, then setup and rocked it. Thanks to all our great friends and fans we won! Winning 90% of the online AND bar crowd vote along with perfect scores from all the judges. It was a good night.

May 4th, Friday, we had the honor of playing the Eliminate Through Music benefit put on by the University of Iowa. Playing alongside Caterwaulla and Intersection, it was a great night. The next day we headed up to Cedar Rapids and partied with good friends John and Matt D at Just Jules with some AWESOME hard rock/metal bands: Dueling at Dawn and Numbered Existence. They both really brought the metal and set the bar pretty high for the night. Check them out. All in all that was one hell of a weekend.

The next weekend would be no different. Thursday we headed up to party at the HuB, we really digg that place as it’s a great venue and some really good friends live close Cody, Sommer, September, etc…. Forgot to mentioned that we played with Jon Wayne and the Pain + Sun Company (Ames). It was SIIIIIICK. Sun Company really laid down the jams moving from one dance-able groove to another with long instrumental breaks in between. It was pretty sick when the keyboard player started using a Melodica! As for Jon Wayne and The Pain, not much to be said. They fuckin’ KILLED it. Their mix of Dub, Reggae, Ska, and electronic really brought the pain and had the whole place dancing. As always it was a good time at the HuB, with a little extra spice from Amo as he was carrying around a boa all night. The people didn’t know what to think. The after party at Sommer’s was, as always, one hell of a time.

The next day we were lucky enough to have our show in West Bend Iowa canceled. No grad party, no long road trip makes for a happy band.

Saturday was the grand finale, and what a finale it was. The Caterwaulla CD release extravaganza. We played with some AWESOME Iowa rock bands that night. Velcro Moxie kicked off the show. This is the first time we’ve played with them and it won’t be the last. Having 2 guitars, both female and male vocals, and a keyboard player, it was a great musical show. I’m sure many people caught themselves singing along as the tunes sank into their heads, I know I did. AsBigAsMouse rocked it hard, remind me of Muddy Rails or Knubby. It heavy, punky, and rockin’, what more could you ask for.  Don’t tell Lauren, but before our set some of the i9 crew snuck over to yacht and saw Zeta June, let me tell you it was a good idea.  If you like Pink Floyd, you better check these guys out.

After we kept the stage warm for Caterwaulla they tore into it hard and rocked the late into the night.  Before I knew it the bar tenders were kicking everyone out and the music was over.  Thankfully, there were kegs and a house to after party at.  It was a LOOONG night.

The Den – Tornadoes – Iowa Rock Music

Posted on May 3, 2012 by Pete9
Reply

Thunderfoot - Facebook

Thunderfoot Rockin The Den in Lone Tree

The Enz – Facebook

The Enz Rockin Tornadoes

Tyler Putz

The Den (Lone Tree Bar)

Tornadoes

This has been a productive weekend for the i9MH crew.

The weekend started out at The Den in Lone Tree on Friday night. Having played with some other great acts we all had a blast. First up, our friend Tyler Putz, played a solo 20 minute set. This was a great preview of what lies ahead in his musical career as he moves to Iowa City this week. Second was the awesome THUNDERFOOT. They really showed the people of Lone Tree what a power trio really is. The entire bar was singing along to songs they were hearing for the first time, it was awesome. As usually Start Wearing Purple is still ringing in all our ears. After Das Thunderfoot we setup and kicked off the set with the James Bond theme, after this we ran through a good number of our originals including the newish biggie jam along with some of the many classic rock songs in our cover arsenal including Rock N Roll, The Ocean, Santaria, and Kryptonite among others. By the end of the night our good friend who helped setup the show, Tyler Telford, was on stage singing with the rest of us. Not to mention Thunderfoot drummer, Alfred, relieving Maxwell of his tambourine duties while trying to take over the mic on She Needs It. It really was another great night at a small town bar, a fun time was had by all.

Saturday did not disappoint either. Tornadoes is definitely one of our favorite venues for live music. This time having the pleasure of playing with The Enz along with Thunderfoot. In typical rock band fashion we showed up a bit later than expected but all was not lost as The Enz showed up early and had a majority of things setup. Once we got there and Jade (from The Enz) setup the drums, the show had begun.

The Enz started their soundcheck with drummer Jade Beardsley on guitar and guitarist Alex Watts on drums. This was quite unexpected but FUCKING AWESOME! After the sound check Alex and Jade switched spots and really rocked it Enz style. Their singer/bass player Spencer Watts (brother to Alex) really moved the crowd and got the Cedar Rapidians rockin. Pluckin’, Slappin’, and Tappin’ the bass he really owned the instrument. Alex and Jade were no different, they really set the tone for the rest of the night. This was the first Enz show since the unfortunate events in November and I can say everyone was honored to be a part of it.

After The Enz Thunderfoot came to rock the stage. In typical fashion the 3 brought it and brought it hard. As a big fan of Five in a Hand, I have to say these guys have the 5inaHand style but with a totally new and distinctive twist.  Another awesome thing about this set, they had a guest musicians play keys and slide guitar on the fly.  He did it all by ear and it sounded great!  If you haven’t seen Thunderfoot live yet, gimme ya badge cuz you really missin out baby. Even if I was deaf, these guys would be one of my favorite bands. Watching them live is one hell of a time.

After these 2 glorious bands played i9MH was up. A little hesitant to play after 2 great sets they got on stage and rocked out to some of the greatest friends and fans a band could have. Pappa Murph even graced the stage with his presence, rocking with item9 to some his favorite originals and covers.

* Good quality photos taken by Kodi McQuillen, THANKS KODI!!!!
