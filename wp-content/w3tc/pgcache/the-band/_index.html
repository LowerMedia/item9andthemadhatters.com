The Band

High Energy Rock N’ Roll

Item 9 & The Mad Hatters hail from Iowa City, Iowa but play their unique blend of rock n roll styles all across the country. This is a show you won’t want to miss. Be it the wailing double lead guitars, the unique but memorable lyrics, the melodic bass drums, tight drumming, or Joe Cocker-esque front man, Item 9 will have you holding onto your seat from the get go.

Item 9 started playing original music in 2010 but dates back a while before that. Over the past 2 years Item 9 has played over 120 shows in 11 states (Iowa, Ohio, Indiana, Illinois, Nebraska, Kansas, Colorado, New Mexico, Arizona, California, and Nevada) and has been featured on a few college and rock radio stations.

 

The Story

 

The story of Item 9 & The Mad Hatters begins in mid-2006 when founder/songwriter, Pete Lower, had a couple experiences in some small bands. The first, a punk/rock trio named Fail. Fail wrote original songs, had a 3 song EP and would eventually break up after 6 months. Currently Item 9 plays White Light from the Fail EP. The other, a cover band titled Old Style, Pete started with his brother Henri Lower and friend Adam Montgomery. As time passed more pieces fell into place, and after playing with multiple other singers and guitarists the band got serious by looking for more consistent members. The band found what they were looking for in Matt Bryks, a good friend, who said when asked if he would like to join a band: “I don’t see why not”. After a successful gig the band knew they had the right line up but still needed a strong frontman. After auditioning several singers the band finally found Adam Maxwell in an unlikely place, craigslist. With all the pieces now in place, the new band was created in January 2010, and forged the name ‘Item 9 & the Mad Hatters’.





By early 2011 Henri decided to take some time off from the band to pursue other interests, and Indiana native Bobby Abrams took over the sticks as dummer in the summer of 2011. The band claims the mean streets of Iowa City, IA as their home. The band has seen rapid growth in their popularity since their arrival in 2010. Playing almost each week locally in the Iowa City/Des Moines/Cedar Falls and surrounding areas. They have seen a substantial increase in the show’s attendance each time they perform outside of their hometown. The growth in attendance is sometimes double or triple than their previous performances. The most the band has performed in front of was at Glowstock in Cedar Falls during the fall of 2011, with about 800 in attendance. They have also traveled across state lines multiple times into Illinois playing Carbondale and performed in Chicago.

In the coming year of 2012 the band is participating in the U-Rock Tour across the United States in select locations like: Chicago, Indianapolis, Los Angeles, and also Las Vegas. They will be playing along side stars like Walle and T.I. In the band’s short existence they have experienced a rapid increase in popularity, and they credit all of that to those who know them most: The Mad Hatters, a term the band has adopted to label to the their fans. From the beginning it has always been about their love of music and the enjoyment they, and the fans, get from hearing their music. The band believes what has gotten them this far and what will help continue their development of success has been the love and devotion they receive from their loyal fans. Item 9’s mission is to continue to be to provide quality rock music to please The Mad Hatters, and to continue doing what they love. It has never been about the money, and the band is based off of love for music and their enjoyment to entertain.
